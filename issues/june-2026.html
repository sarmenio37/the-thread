This Month's Updates
Caroline
📍 Bend, Oregon
🏔️
✨ Highlight: Survived a living room reno, a toddler tornado, and am gearing up for a rainbow-themed birthday bash — all while job hunting!

Still pondering whether we're training a future overlord to become sentient through this exercise — but I digress. This month has been a wild ride, equal parts insane and surreal. When did I morph into a fully-fledged adult? Transitioning to life with two kids under two is going well, though it's not for the faint of heart. My nervous system, despite years of therapy, seems to thrive on stress, which is lucky because there's plenty of it to go around! Remi is gaining independence and language skills at his own pace, which keeps us on our toes. And despite my best efforts, Randy's 'projects leave' turned into my nightmare of a construction zone with a newborn in tow. But hey, at least our living room looks fab now — minus a few missing shelves and light fixtures. Next time, I'm lobbying for 'bonding with baby' leave!

Our childcare situation is a bit of a circus, with our nanny needing more time off than we can manage. It's a tough spot between empathy for her and the reality of needing reliable help. Meanwhile, Randy's first week back at work was met with a snot-filled toddler and a missing nanny, which stressed him out to no end. But hey, it's not all doom and gloom. Mac is growing like a weed, laughing, rolling over, and eating only eight times a day now — progress! Remi is a curious big brother, and his 'gentle' hugs are getting gentler by the day. We've even managed a hike along Tumalo Creek, which was pure magic for my soul.

We're also gearing up for Remi's second birthday party, ambitiously themed "TWO much fun" at a play gym, promising toddler chaos in the best way. Meanwhile, I'm embracing wide-leg pants post-baby and may even land a full-time gig with HOKA — because, why not add a job hunt to the mix? Summer's here, and our backyard is a toy-strewn paradise. Remi's living his best life with his baby pool, water table, and soon-to-arrive playhouse.

Through all this, I'm learning the art of planning with two little ones, or rather, the impossibility of it. I fantasize about fun outings and friend meetups, only to realize the logistical nightmare of 2 under 2. So, I'm thrilled about our upcoming August visit and officially propose we start plotting our 40th birthday trip. Let's kick off with the where and when, shall we?

✨ Highlight: Survived a leveling project, now ready for Post Malone's dance moves and Elsa's magic at Irie's birthday bash!

On the way to the lake, we're all cramming into a cozy two-bedroom, one-bath cottage — the four of us plus Mawmaw. Sounds like the perfect setting to crack open some Busch Lights, if you ask me. Next week, I'm off to see Post Malone with a bunch of friends. Honestly, I'm not even a huge fan, but I'm here for the dance moves. Then it's a nostalgia trip back to 2008 with Lil Wayne and my buddy Linds Mo. Can't wait!

We had to tear up our whole landscape and gutter drainage, redo it all, and dump a truckload of dirt around the foundation. But hey, my garden and compost are up and running again — slowly bringing my hillbilly roots back.

We're throwing a princess birthday party for Irie, and Elsa is making an appearance. I'm so pumped, and I know Irie will be thrilled. She's really into fishing right now and even won a tournament — her first trophy and medal! Meanwhile, Mavery is in the middle of learning to twerk. She gets halfway up and just bounces, which is fantastic. She's starting to talk too, with words like 'ball' (or 'bouwwwllllll,' as she says it), 'cheers,' and 'I-I-I-rie.' Miss you guys and can't wait to catch up soon!

Sarah
📍 Cleveland, Ohio
⚽
✨ Highlight: From pierogies in Poland to salsa in Puerto Rico, June's been a wild ride!

June has been a whirlwind of travel, work, and a few too many airport lounges. I kicked off the month at communityLive in Orlando, leading panels and connecting with customers. My suitcase and I had a bit of a falling out, thanks to some unfortunate travel hiccups, but nothing a little Polish beer and pierogies in Krakow couldn't fix. Afterward, I found myself salsa dancing in Puerto Rico — not a bad way to end a busy month.

On the home front, Chris has been an all-star, keeping things running smoothly while I hopscotch across time zones. Vela graduated to a 'big girl' bed, and we passed her crib onto Jule and Dewey for their little one. I'm cherishing some downtime now, planning a trip to the Columbus Zoo with Vela and catching up with Kyla and Irie. We've also got a few home improvement projects underway — one ceiling at a time!

Family life is thriving, with Vela's swimming lessons still a Friday highlight. We celebrated Cookie's birthday, enjoyed some quality pool time, and I managed a breakfast date with my sisters and mom. It's been a tiring but fulfilling month, and I'm savoring every chaotic, beautifully imperfect moment.

A note from all three of us

As we navigate through the chaos of construction zones, toddler antics, and globetrotting adventures, it seems we're all mastering the art of thriving amidst life's beautifully orchestrated chaos. From wide-leg pants to nostalgic tunes and a sprinkle of Polish beer, we're embracing the unpredictable and savoring every moment. Here's to our shared love of laughter, adventure, and a good plan (even if it never quite goes as expected).
