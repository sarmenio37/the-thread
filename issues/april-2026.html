This Month's Updates
Caroline
📍 Bend, Oregon
🏔️
✨ Highlight: Finally had my solo ski day and discovered recipes actually work — who knew?

April finally decided to act like spring around here in Bend, which means everyone and their mother is outside, whether it's biking, hiking, or splashing around in the Deschutes. I even managed to squeeze in that elusive solo ski day I kept pushing off — and let me tell you, it was worth every 'I'll do it next weekend' I muttered under my breath all season.

Work has been its own weird cocktail of busy and slow. I’ve got a new project in the works that I’m cautiously excited about. But I'm trying not to get too ahead of myself — I mean, I’ve learned from experience that manifesting too hard just invites chaos. On the culinary front, I've started cooking more seriously. Who knew following recipes could actually make things taste good? It's like magic, but with less wand-waving and more measuring cups.

I miss you both terribly. I've found myself daydreaming about that week at the lake and feeling the urge to plan something equally memorable before summer slips through our fingers like a toddler with sunscreen.

Kyla
📍 Columbus, Ohio
🌷
✨ Highlight: Wore shorts and a coat in the same week, read three books, and kept a plant alive — personal growth?

Ah, Columbus in April — where you can experience all four seasons without leaving the week. I've somehow managed to wear both a coat and shorts in the same 48-hour span, not once, but twice. It's a talent, really.

In other news, I've rekindled my love affair with fiction, managing to devour three books in three weeks. It's a personal record I'm quite proud of, especially since I had been lost in the non-fiction woods, trying to 'smarten up' about systems beyond my control. Turns out, they were just stressing me out. Fiction, it seems, is the way to go for now.

Oh, and Gerald the pothos is alive and thriving! This is monumental, given my track record with plants. It feels like a metaphor for personal growth or something equally profound — who knew I'd find life lessons in a potted plant?

Sarah
📍 Cleveland, Ohio
⚽
✨ Highlight: Survived the last Dyngus Day, started house hunting, and Vela's on the loose with her birthday scooter!

April has been a whirlwind, starting with what might be our last Dyngus Day — a vibrant mishmash of food, polka, and friends. Vela, our little water warrior, had a blast sprinting around the backyard with her squirt gun. I've got a bunch of pictures to share, capturing the joy and chaos of it all.

We’ve embarked on the adventure of house hunting in Brecksville. While we considered half-day preschools for the fall, we've decided to keep Vela with Sammi, our amazing nanny, until kindergarten. In the meantime, Vela is enjoying the newfound freedom of her birthday scooter as the weather finally decided to cooperate.

Work remains a balancing act — busy yet rewarding. With Jules and Dewey on parental leave, I’m juggling a bit more, but the team is settling into a groove. There’s a looming change on the horizon with my boss getting a new boss, so there may be some interesting shifts ahead. I’m gearing up for more travel with upcoming customer visits and our big conference, CommunityLive, in May and June.

We’re looking forward to summer, wrapping up house projects, and sneaking in some weekend escapes with Vela and Chris. And, of course, the boat will make its seasonal debut in May!

A note from all three of us

As April showers us with its usual blend of chaos and charm, we find ourselves navigating new beginnings with a shared sense of cautious optimism—whether it's Caroline's culinary magic, Kyla's fictional escapes, or Sarah's house-hunting adventures. Here's to embracing the unpredictable, finding growth in unexpected places, and plotting summer escapes that promise laughter, love, and maybe a little less sunscreen in our eyes.
