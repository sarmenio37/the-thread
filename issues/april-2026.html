This Month's Updates
Caroline
📍 Bend, Oregon
🏔️
✨ Highlight: Finally went skiing solo and didn't get lost — miracles do happen!

April has finally sprung into spring here in Bend, which means everyone is immediately outside, doing their best impersonations of Lance Armstrong on bikes, hitting the trails, or splashing around in the Deschutes. I finally went on that solo ski day I'd been postponing all season, and let me tell you, it was worth every single 'I'll do it next weekend' I muttered to myself.

Work has been this bizarre cocktail of busy and slow — kind of like trying to mix a kale smoothie with a shot of espresso. There's a new project on the horizon that I'm cautiously excited about, but I'm trying my best not to jinx it by manifesting anything just yet. Speaking of new adventures, I've started cooking more seriously — as in, actually following recipes instead of my usual freestyling that often leaves me wondering why dinner tastes suspiciously like regret.

I miss you both dearly. Lately, I'm finding myself daydreaming about that glorious week at the lake and itching to plan something real before summer sneaks away from us faster than a toddler with a cookie.

Kyla
📍 Columbus, Ohio
🌷
✨ Highlight: Wore shorts and a coat in 48 hours, devoured three books, and somehow kept Gerald the pothos alive!

Columbus has been up to its usual antics, serving up all four seasons in a single week. I've found myself donning a coat and shorts within the same 48 hours—not once, but twice. It's as if the weather is trying to keep us on our toes, or at least in perpetual wardrobe confusion.

I've dived headfirst into a reading spree, polishing off three books in just as many weeks. It's a personal record for me. I had been on a non-fiction kick, thinking it would make me smarter, but it mostly just made me anxious about systems beyond my control. Fiction feels like a much-needed escape and a reminder that not everything has to be so serious.

In a plot twist for the books, Gerald the pothos is not just surviving but thriving! I've never managed to keep a plant alive before, so this feels like a symbolic victory for personal growth. Maybe I'm finally cracking the code on this whole 'nurturing' thing.

Sarah
📍 Cleveland, Ohio
⚽
✨ Highlight: Survived what might be our final Dyngus Day and kicked off house hunting adventures!

April was a whirlwind of activity, starting with what might be our last Dyngus Day celebration. It was a blast of food, polka, and friends, with Vela enthusiastically leading the charge with her trusty squirt gun in the backyard. I have some pictures that capture the chaos perfectly, and I'll send them your way.

On the home front, we've officially started house hunting in the Brecksville area. We also explored a few half-day preschools for Vela this fall, but decided to stick with our wonderful nanny, Sammi, until kindergarten calls. The nice weather has finally arrived, and Vela is loving her birthday scooter, zipping around with glee.

Work is its usual mix of busy and engaging, with a sprinkle of headaches for flavor. I'm settling well into the team, though my boss is getting a new boss, so change is on the horizon. With Jules and Dewey on maternity/paternity leave, I'm juggling a bit more, especially as I gear up for our company's CommunityLive conference in May and June.

As we look forward to summer, we're planning some weekend escapes with Vela and Chris, finishing a few house projects, and, excitingly, getting the boat in the water by May. Here's to sunny days and smooth sailing ahead!

A note from all three of us

As April showers us with its signature mix of chaos and change, we've each embraced our own little victories—Caroline's culinary conquests, Kyla's blossoming green thumb, and Sarah's adventurous house hunt. While the weather keeps us guessing, it's comforting to know that our friendship remains the most reliable constant, a steady thread through our seasons of life. Here's to more impromptu adventures and the hope that our next gathering is as delightful as Caroline's kale-espresso smoothies are intriguing!
