This Month's Updates
Caroline
📍 Bend, Oregon
🏔️
✨ Highlight: Finally got that solo ski day and started following recipes—who knew instructions were useful?

April finally decided to stop being winter-ish and started acting like spring here in Bend, which, of course, means the entire town collectively dashed outside. Whether it's on bikes, trails, or daring the Deschutes, we're out there. I finally managed to sneak in that elusive solo ski day I've been promising myself all season, and let me tell you, it was worth every "I'll do it next weekend" procrastination.

Work is doing its best impression of a moody teenager—sometimes frantically busy, sometimes alarmingly slow. I'm cautiously excited about a new project but trying not to jinx it by getting too enthusiastic just yet. In the culinary department, I've started to actually follow recipes instead of my usual 'wing it and hope for the best' approach. Turns out, things taste better when you don't improvise like a jazz musician who can't play an instrument.

I miss you both so much. I've been reminiscing about that week at the lake and thinking we should plan something real before summer slips through our fingers faster than a toddler with a popsicle.

Kyla
📍 Columbus, Ohio
🌷
✨ Highlight: Wore a coat and shorts in one week, read three books, and Gerald the pothos is still alive—it's a season of miracles!

Columbus has been doing its classic "four seasons in one week" thing. I've somehow managed to wear both a coat and shorts within the same 48-hour window. Not once, but twice. It's like a climate-based wardrobe workout.

Lately, I've been in a reading frenzy—three books in three weeks. It feels like a personal best. I had been deep in the non-fiction trenches, thinking I was getting smarter, but really, I was just getting anxious about systems I can't control. Fiction has been a much-needed escape.

And in other news, Gerald the pothos is absolutely thriving. This feels important because historically, my track record with plants has been... well, let's just say Gerald's success might be a sign of personal growth. Literally.

Sarah
📍 Cleveland, Ohio
⚽
✨ Highlight: Survived Dyngus Day with a splash and started house hunting — onwards to new adventures!

April has been a whirlwind, starting with what might just be our last Dyngus Day celebration! Picture it: a backyard, full of friends, polka music, and Vela sprinting around with a squirt gun like some kind of water-wielding ninja. I've got a few photos to share from the day, and they're sure to bring a smile.

In other exciting news, we've embarked on the adventure of house hunting in Brecksville. It's a bit like dating, but with more paperwork and less small talk. We're also looking at preschools for Vela but decided to stick with our wonderful nanny, Sammi, until kindergarten calls.

Work has been its usual busy self — engaging and occasionally headache-inducing. With my boss getting a new boss, things are bound to shake up a bit. I'm gearing up for more travel come May and June, particularly for our company conference, CommunityLive. Covering for my teammates Jules and Dewey while they're on parental leave promises to keep things interesting.

Sunny weather has finally graced us, and Vela has been zipping around on her birthday scooter. We're looking forward to getting the boat in the water in May and squeezing in some weekend trips with Chris. Summer, here we come!

A note from all three of us

As April showers bring a kaleidoscope of weather and personal growth, we find ourselves in a dance with change, both in our closets and in life. Whether it's Caroline's culinary adventures, Kyla's literary escapes, or Sarah's house-hunting escapades, we're all navigating new projects and passions with a touch of springtime whimsy. Here's to embracing the unexpected and planning our next great escape, before the summer sun takes the lead in our seasonal symphony.
