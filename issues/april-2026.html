This Month's Updates
Caroline
📍 Bend, Oregon
🏔️
✨ Highlight: First solo ski day of the season

April finally started feeling like spring out here — which in Bend means everyone is immediately outside, on bikes, on trails, in the Deschutes. I finally got that solo ski day I'd been putting off all season and it was worth every "I'll do it next weekend."

Work has been a weird mix of busy and slow. New project that I'm cautiously excited about but trying not to manifest anything just yet. Also started cooking more seriously — like, actually following recipes instead of freestyling and wondering why things taste wrong.

I miss you both. Finding myself thinking about that week at the lake and wanting to plan something real before the summer gets away from us.

🏔️

Kyla
📍 Columbus, Ohio
🌷
✨ Highlight: Adopted a plant. Named him Gerald.

Columbus has been doing its classic "four seasons in one week" thing. I've worn a coat and shorts in the same 48-hour window. Twice.

I've been in a real reading phase — finished three books in three weeks, which feels like a personal record. Mostly fiction, which I'd abandoned for a while in favor of non-fiction that I thought was making me smarter but was mostly just making me anxious about systems I can't control.

Also: Gerald the pothos is thriving. This feels significant. I have never successfully kept a plant alive before. Personal growth, I think.

🌿
📚
Sarah
📍 Cleveland, Ohio
⚽
✨ Highlight: Memoir pages are officially happening.

Work has been big in a good-stressful way. There's a project that could actually change how a hospital system runs its operations — the kind of thing that sounds boring until you're in the room watching people realize how much time they've been losing to friction that didn't have to exist.

On the personal side: I've been carving out Sunday mornings for the memoir. It's slow, but it's moving. Turns out writing about your own life requires that you actually look at it, which is either the point or the problem.

Weather finally turned. Took a long walk by the lake and remembered why living here is actually fine. More than fine.

✍️
🌊

A note from all three of us

Three cities, three different springs, one thread. What strikes us reading back through these updates is how much is happening quietly — the books, the cooking, the writing, the small things that don't make calendar alerts but make up most of a life. We're here for all of it.
