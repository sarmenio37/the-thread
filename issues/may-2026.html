This Month's Updates
Caroline
📍 Bend, Oregon
🏔️
✨ Highlight: Still waiting for Baby Number Two and waddling around like a determined penguin on a hike!

Well, folks, it seems that procrastination is still my strong suit because here I am, staring at the blank form with all the intensity of a hiker spotting a bear on the trail. You'd think by the second child, I'd have this whole 'getting things done' business down, but alas, here I am. Speaking of which, we're still waiting for Baby Number Two to make their grand entrance. I've been waddling around the house like a penguin on a mission, desperately trying to remember what it feels like to have ankles.

In other news, my attempts at staying healthy have reached new heights—or should I say new trails? I took on another hike last week, which was less of a serene nature walk and more of a comedy sketch. Imagine me, belly and all, attempting to gracefully navigate rocks and roots. Spoiler alert: grace was not achieved, but laughter definitely was. It's amazing how much you can bond with strangers when you're all collectively trying not to faceplant.

On the home front, I've been nesting like a bird on a caffeine high, organizing and reorganizing everything in sight. If you need any advice on folding baby clothes into perfect little burritos, I'm your gal. I might not have it all together, but at least my nursery does. So here's to May, where the wild adventures of motherhood continue, and my sense of humor remains my best survival tool.

Kyla
📍 Columbus, Ohio
🌷
✨ Highlight: Mavery's dancing, Irie's spelling, and I'm pumped for Sonic Temple — lit, right?

I think Mavery is about to start walking in the next couple of weeks — cue the tears! She's all about dancing these days, which basically means flinging her arms up like the true millennial offspring she is and swaying with the biggest grin. She's got a special wave for birds that's more like telling a DJ to 'bump it,' and it's hilarious. Dogs are her absolute favorite, and she's the best sleeper ever. So calm, so happy. Seriously, she's the sweetest.

Irie is blowing my mind with her smarts. She's spelling words and doing addition — I'm not kidding. She's gearing up to graduate pre-K in June and will be reading a poem at the ceremony, so we've been practicing like crazy. Brandon and I met with her future kindergarten teachers last week and nearly had a heart attack over their schedule. So many days off! Can't believe she's starting actual school in just four months. It's wild. She's acing swim class, and that's about all we have time for these days.

We had a kid-free blast at my cousin's wedding last weekend, but spent all of Sunday recovering. Woof. We're also on the hunt for a contractor to build us a back deck that doesn't cost a million bucks. Brandon and I are heading to Sonic Temple soon (you might remember it as Rock on the Range) to relive our early 2000s glory days with bands like Sublime, Yellowcard, and New Found Glory. I am absolutely pumped (lit?) for that!

No vacation plans this year, which is a bummer. Our next big trips are Costa Rica and Disney, but not for a while. I'm planning a Florida trip next spring with my high school friends for our 40ths. And maybe we'll get something on the books for next year, too. It's a BIG year. Also thinking about a gameday party for my birthday, but haven't had time to plan it yet. You'll both be invited, but no expectations — just good vibes.

Sarah
📍 Cleveland, Ohio
⚽
✨ Highlight: Earned a promotion, Vela met Elsa, and our boat took its first 2026 dip — all in a May's work!

May felt like an intense prelude to the summer symphony. Work was front and center, mostly because June is shaping up to be a travel bonanza. I got promoted to Director of Product Management, which feels like a big deal, even though my imposter syndrome would have you believe otherwise. I had a whirlwind trip to Rochester, NY, for a presentation and then dove into a professional development week. Oh, and it looks like a new boss is on the horizon — an SVP — so that's going to be interesting.

On the personal front, I embarked on a Memoir Workshop journey. We meet weekly, and it's been a real eye-opener into the art of turning memories into something coherent and hopefully compelling. My goal is to have a full draft by the end of the year, so fingers crossed I can keep the momentum. Vela, on the other hand, is living her best life. We went to a princess tea party where she met Elsa, and her excitement was off the charts. She's diving into swim classes too, with Chris taking her on Fridays.

Mother's Day was a family affair with visits to Cookie and my mom. Our boat finally made its splashy debut in the water, though it still needs a bit of TLC before it's lake-ready. I managed a comedy night out with my sister and nieces, which was a much-needed laugh fest. And last week, Vela and I ventured to Memphis Kiddie Park, soaking up the elusive warmth of May. Flowers are in bloom, and it feels like the world is waking up again.

A note from all three of us

As May unfolds, it seems we're all dancing to our own unique rhythms—Caroline's comedic trail blazers, Kyla's toddler twirls, and Sarah's balancing act between imposter syndrome and well-deserved recognition. Perhaps the real thread here is our shared journey of navigating life’s bustling adventures with humor, resilience, and a touch of nostalgia, all while keeping our hearts open to the beauty of what's next. Here's to embracing the chaos and finding joy in every step, stumble, and dance move along the way!
